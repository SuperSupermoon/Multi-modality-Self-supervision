What was previously confluent and symmetric consolidation in both lungs is now more heterogeneous, and in some areas frankly nodular, with possible cavitation. These findings would be concerning for disseminated infection, either pneumonia or septic emboli. However in light of the late provided history of massive blood aspiration and equally massive transfusion, we may be seeing the asymmetric clearing of aspirated blood in the setting of edema due to blood product administration. Moderate cardiomegaly and mediastinal vascular engorgement have improved. ET tube and right internal jugular venous line are in standard placements and an esophageal tube passes into the stomach and out of view. No pneumothorax or appreciable pleural effusion.
