PORT-A-CATH CATHETER TIP TERMINATES IN THE CAVOATRIAL JUNCTION OR PROXIMAL RIGHT ATRIUM. IT LOOPS DURING ITS COURSE AND 1 OF THE TRIBUTARIES OF THE BRACHIAL CEPHALIC VEIN, UNCHANGED IN APPEARANCE. THERE IS INTERVAL DEVELOPMENT OF RIGHT BASILAR OPACITY AS WELL AS OVERALL VASCULAR ENGORGEMENT THAT ARE CONCERNING FOR INTERVAL DEVELOPMENT OF INTERSTITIAL PULMONARY EDEMA. INFECTIOUS PROCESS IS LESS LIKELY. THERE IS ALSO INTERVAL INCREASE IN RIGHT PLEURAL EFFUSION. THERE IS NO PNEUMOTHORAX.
