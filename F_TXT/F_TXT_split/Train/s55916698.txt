Bilateral asymmetrical airspace disease, which may reflect asymmetrical cardiogenic pulmonary edema from postpartum cardiomyopathy or from a pre-existing cardiac condition. Differential diagnosis for diffuse airspace opacities in the early post-partum period also includes amniotic fluid embolism, aspiration pneumonia, and widespread infectious pneumonia. Correlation with cardiac echo may be helpful for initial further evaluation if warranted clinically.
