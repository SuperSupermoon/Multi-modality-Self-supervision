Significant bibasilar atelectasis, right greater than left developed between ___ and ___. Subsequently and mild interstitial abnormality on ___ has gotten much more pronounced. The rapid development and relatively symmetric distribution, in a perihilar and mildly basal predominant fashion, are most typical of pulmonary edema, and the concurrent increase in caliber of mediastinal veins suggests either biventricular heart failure or volume overload or both, even though the heart, slightly larger than before, is not enlarged. Pneumonia is not excluded but does not need to be invoked to explain these findings
